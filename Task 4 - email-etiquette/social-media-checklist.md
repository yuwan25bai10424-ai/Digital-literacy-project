# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible digital presence matters — especially when you're building your academic and professional reputation. Here's a practical checklist to guide your social media use.

---

## ✅ DO's — Responsible Social Media Habits

1. **Think before you post.**
   Always pause and ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, future employer, or family saw this?" If the answer is no, don't post it.

2. **Keep your profiles professional where it counts.**
   Platforms like LinkedIn should reflect your academic achievements, skills, and goals. Update your bio, add your projects, and engage with industry content.

3. **Fact-check before sharing.**
   Verify information from credible sources before resharing news, statistics, or claims. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — affects your credibility.

4. **Respect privacy — yours and others'.**
   Avoid sharing personal details like your home address, daily schedule, or location in real time. Always ask for permission before posting photos or tagging others.

5. **Use social media for learning and networking.**
   Follow educators, thought leaders, and companies in your field. Engage meaningfully in discussions, comment thoughtfully, and build genuine professional connections.

6. **Be kind and constructive online.**
   Treat others with the same respect you'd expect in person. Constructive disagreement is healthy; personal attacks are not.

7. **Keep your security settings updated.**
   Regularly review privacy settings on all platforms. Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts — Habits to Avoid

1. **Don't post content you'll regret later.**
   Deleted posts can still be screenshotted and shared. Offensive, inappropriate, or impulsive posts can follow you for years and affect job applications, college recommendations, and relationships.

2. **Don't share copyrighted content without credit.**
   Using someone else's images, music, or writing without permission or attribution is unethical and can have legal consequences.

3. **Don't engage in or tolerate cyberbullying.**
   Avoid posting content that demeans, mocks, or targets individuals. If you witness cyberbullying, don't amplify it — report it.

4. **Don't overshare personal or academic information.**
   Avoid posting exam schedules, assignment details, or institutional passwords. This protects both your academic integrity and personal security.

5. **Don't let social media consume your study time.**
   Set screen time limits and designated study blocks. Constant notifications and doomscrolling can significantly impact your academic performance and mental health.

---

*Remember: Your digital footprint is a reflection of your character. Build it with intention.*
