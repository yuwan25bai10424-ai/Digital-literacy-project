# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Chosen Crime Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud

---

## What Is It?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where scammers trick people into sending money or sharing sensitive credentials through India's digital payment platforms like PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, or BHIM. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI fraud often exploits human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities — making it one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India today.

---

## How It Typically Happens — Step by Step

1. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, WhatsApp, or SMS, often posing as a bank official, customer care executive, or a buyer on OLX/Quikr.

2. **Building Trust:** They create urgency — "Your account will be blocked," "I want to buy your item, please share your UPI ID," or "You've won a cashback reward."

3. **The Trick:** The scammer sends a *collect request* (a payment request, not a payment) on UPI apps. Victims, confused by the interface, enter their UPI PIN thinking they're *receiving* money — but they're actually *approving a payment out.*

4. **Money Lost:** The moment the victim enters their PIN, funds are instantly transferred. UPI transactions are irreversible.

5. **Disappearance:** The scammer blocks the victim and vanishes, often using a fake or burner SIM card.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- **First-time digital payment users**, especially in semi-urban and rural areas
- **Senior citizens** unfamiliar with UPI collect request mechanics
- **Online sellers** on platforms like OLX who receive fake "payment confirmation" screenshots
- **Job seekers** lured by work-from-home scams requiring a "registration fee"
- **Students** promised scholarships or part-time income

---

## Real-World Fictional Scenario

Priya, a 58-year-old teacher from Bhopal, listed her old sofa on OLX. A "buyer" called, said he'd pay via Google Pay, and sent her a ₹15,000 collect request. He told her to "accept the payment by entering your PIN." Priya did so — and lost ₹15,000 in seconds. The buyer's number was switched off immediately after.

*(Fictional scenario based on commonly reported fraud patterns in India)*

---

## Consequences

- **Financial loss** — often ranging from ₹1,000 to several lakhs, with little chance of recovery
- **Emotional trauma** — shame, anxiety, and loss of confidence in digital payments
- **Legal complexity** — victims struggle to file effective complaints due to lack of awareness
- **Economic impact** — erodes public trust in India's digital payment ecosystem (UPI processed over ₹18 lakh crore in transactions in 2023)
